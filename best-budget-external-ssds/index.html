Quick answer

The Kingston Fury Beast Pro 2TB delivers the best combination of speed and affordability for everyday users.

The ADATA SD700 Rugged 2TB is ideal if you need a tough, weather-resistant drive, while the Seagate Barracuda Fast SSD 2TB suits those who want premium build and backup software.

External solid-state drives have become genuinely affordable, making them a smart upgrade over thumb drives or portable hard drives for anyone who moves large files regularly. Whether you are backing up photos, storing game libraries, or editing video on the go, an SSD gives you much faster transfers and better shock resistance than a spinning hard drive, and at prices that no longer require a big budget. When choosing a budget external SSD, look for a balance of read/write speed (USB 3.2 Gen 2 or better is ideal), physical durability if you travel often, and any included backup or security software. Capacity is also key: 2TB is the sweet spot for most people, offering enough room for hundreds of games or tens of thousands of photos without breaking the bank. The three drives here all hit that capacity and represent the best values at different priorities.

Best for everyday speed and value

Our verdict

For the vast majority of people looking to spend a reasonable amount on a portable 2TB SSD, the Kingston Fury Beast Pro is the clear winner: it delivers excellent real-world speed at a very competitive price. If you need something that can take a beating, the ADATA SD700 Rugged is a trustworthy companion. And if you value a premium feel together with robust backup software and data recovery, the Seagate Barracuda Fast SSD is worth the extra spend, it feels like a long-term investment in your digital safety. Whichever you choose, you are getting a solid, modern drive that makes a hard drive feel archaic.
