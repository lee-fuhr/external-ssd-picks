Best external SSDs for 2026

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

The Samsung T7 Shield 2TB is the best external SSD for most people thanks to its rugged build, fast performance, and broad compatibility.

If you want a slightly more affordable option with near-identical speeds, the SanDisk Extreme Portable SSD is a close runner-up, while the Crucial X9 Pro is ideal for gamers who need optimized console performance.

Portable external SSDs have become essential gear for photographers, videographers, gamers, and anyone who frequently moves large files between devices. Unlike old spinning hard drives, SSDs offer lightning-fast transfers, silent operation, and better shock resistance, making them perfect for on-the-go backup, editing off a drive, or expanding a console’s library. The best models now deliver read/write speeds well over 1 GB/s in a pocket-sized package, and many come with IP-rated water and dust resistance. To pick the right one for you, start by considering the interface: USB 3.2 Gen 2×2 and Thunderbolt 3/4 offer the fastest speeds but require compatible ports. Next, think about durability, do you need a rubberized, drop-proof case or just a slim aluminium enclosure? Finally, decide on capacity (1TB, 2TB, or more) and your budget. The options below represent the top-rated models across different priorities, from all-around excellence to rugged toughness and gaming value.

Best for specific needs

Best for high-speed file transfers

If your workflow revolves around moving large video files, RAW photo libraries, or massive project folders, raw speed is king. The Samsung T7 Shield and SanDisk Extreme both hit roughly 1 GB/s reads and writes, making them ideal for creative professionals. The Samsung edges ahead with slightly better sustained performance and a higher IP rating. Our pick: Samsung T7 Shield 2TB.

Best for durability on the go

When your drive needs to survive drops into mud, splashes, or even full submersion, the ADATA SD700 Rugged is the clear winner. Its IP68 rating means it can handle being dunked in a meter of water for 30 minutes, and the shock-absorbing case protects against falls. It’s slower than NVMe drives, but reliability in tough conditions matters more than sheer speed. Our pick: ADATA SD700 Rugged 2TB.

Best for gaming consoles

Modern games can take up hundreds of gigabytes, and a fast external SSD cuts load times dramatically compared to an internal hard drive. The Crucial X9 Pro is optimized for this use case, with support for PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S off-console playback. It’s compact, runs cool, and offers consistent performance without breaking the bank. Our pick: Crucial X9 Pro 2TB.

Our verdict

For most people, the Samsung T7 Shield 2TB remains the smartest all-around choice: it’s fast, durable, and widely compatible. If you want to save a few dollars, the SanDisk Extreme Portable SSD is almost its equal. Gamers should gravitate toward the Crucial X9 Pro, and anyone working in rough conditions will appreciate the tank-like ADATA SD700 Rugged. The Kingston Fury Beast Pro is a great fallback for those on a strict budget who still want SSD speeds. No matter which you choose, any of these drives will be a massive upgrade over a traditional external hard drive.
